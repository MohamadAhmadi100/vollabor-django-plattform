5. On the next page, enter your “New password” and “New password confirmation” and click the “Reset password” button.


6. Now you can enter your account with the new password.

Director

The project director leads the entire project section and makes the final decision on the acceptance or rejection of the project.

Director assistance

The project director assistance observes the experts and helps the director to manage the project section. S/he is a substitute of the director.

Expert

The expert is selected to monitor and handle the entire project process. Each project is assigned to an expert. This expert leads the project from start to end. Each project-related issue is investigated by the expert. If a major problem arises, the expert should refer to the director or assistance. Each expert must undertake several projects each year and report them to the director or assistance on a regular basis.

Members

TECVICO Sectors

TECVICO has three main sectors:

1. Project: It is the fundamental sector. To join TECVICO, all researchers initially need to apply for this sector and after working in this sector and being qualified (obtaining Responsiveness Fee and Badges) they can join other sectors. The main goal of this sector is to find new challenges and scientifically tackle them. The main outcome of this sector is journal and conference publications. Besides focusing on research projects and publications, this sector works with interns to train them for their future careers.

What sector do we start from?

The entrance gate is the project sector. After working in this sector and being qualified by gaining enough Responsiveness Fee and Badges, researchers can join other sectors.

Apply to industry projects

By working in the project sector and acquiring enough Responsiveness Fee and Badges, a researcher will be eligible to join the industrial sector and projects. Obviously, the requirement Res Fee for project members would be different in a way that higher level members need more Res Fees.

Tecvico (Technological Virtual Collaboration) is a Canadian international company founded in Vancouver in 2020. It aims to facilitate the connection between industry and researchers/experts. Tecvico headquarter is located in Canada, and its researchers are collaborating virtually from different countries, such as Portugal, Iran, Canada, Germany, India, and other countries. All levels of researchers (beginner, intermediate, and advanced) can join Tecvico and apply for projects. Current projects are mainly defined in the fields of Artificial Intelligence such as Image Processing, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. In addition, Tecvico is interested to work in other areas, such as Cryptography, Internet of Things (IoT), Cloud Computing, etc. There are three components to each project: • Response points • Experience points •Financial value To enter the project, individuals must have earned response points and experience points required for the project. Response points can be earned by cooperating in other activities of the company or by making a financial commitment to the company. Experience points are earned based on your resume and interview and the activities you do at the company. The outputs of a project are the response score, the experience score, and the amount of the project budget, which is divided proportionally among the people participating in the project. If the project has another product such as an article, the points obtained from it will be awarded to its participants.

Entry permissions

Now, we reached the interesting part of TECVICO, Entry permissions!! Entry permissions are entry permits for projects. They are divided into two main classes:

1. Responsiveness Fee (Res Fee):

This indicates how much a member was responsible for projects that he/she already attended. It contains various aspects, such as attending all weekly meetings, being on-time to deliver a task, being responsible for his/her words and behavior, etc. Depending on the responsibility of researchers in different projects, they are assigned points by projects' supervisors. These points could be converted to Responsiveness Fee. If a researcher cannot afford to pay Responsiveness Fee, he/she can work for TECVICO as an intern or a trial member for a while and after gaining enough points, he/she can join a project.

2. Badges :

This Badges shows how much a member knows a subject and how much he/she is skilled in programming, using software and tools. Depending on how much members were successful to use their skills and knowledge to proceed on a project, they are given points. These points are given by supervisors. Based on these points, researchers are awarded Badges. These Badges cannot be traded, and are only acquired through projects. They play a crucial role in getting into Competition and Industry projects. If a member wants more Ress Fee to join projects with more funds, it is recommended to choose projects that suit his/her abilities and complete them in the best possible way.More effort, More (Ress Fee)!

TECVICO is divided into two sections:

Now, we reached the interesting part of TECVICO, Entry permissions!! Entry permissions are entry permits for projects. They are divided into two main classes:

1. Project section

Projects basically are referred to this section. Some time, small industrial projects will also be associated with this section. To join TECVICO, all applicants must apply for some projects in this section unless they are able to receive a minimum knowledge coin via the interview process. After passing this section and qualifying (obtaining responsibility and knowledge coins), applicants can join other sections. TECVICO invites international laboratories, university professors, industry companies, governments, factories etc. to give the project to us. In Particular, we will provide the university professors and lab supervisors with experts. As it is obvious, the professors are undertaking some big projects from industries or governments. They divide the project into smaller parts and then hire some students and researchers to do it. The divisions most often include various fields or interdisciplinary projects, and the lab members might not be expert in some fields. In this case, the supervisor can give a section of the project to TECVICO. Thus , TECVICO promises to provide the project with experts and lead it to be accomplished. Sometimes, a project is assigned to a researcher/student and s/he is not able to accomplish it. In this case, TECVICO can collaborate with the lab and provide the group with a professional teammate. After completing the project, the members will be provided with i) responsibility coins, ii) knowledge coins, and iii) wage ($) (if the project was already funded). If the project also results in an article, the members will be considered as the authors based on their effort and supervisor’s/company’s rule.

2. Workshop section:

This section offers a platform to hold global or local workshops. It also creates an environment for interested people to follow and participate in advanced workshops. It also supports experienced scientists to transform their knowledge to other people worldwide as well as facilitating the related workshops.

Structure of members in a project

Each project includes 4 categories as follows:

  • 1)Supervisors/advisors
  • 2)Mentor
  • 3)Member
  • 4)Learner
Each project includes at least a supervisor. Multiple advisors can apply for this project. At the end, the first supervisor will select the best advisors and supervisors. Also, multiple members, mentors and learners can request a project to join in. Thus, the first supervisor will select the individuals based on their knowledge and responsibility. The supervisor and advisors lead the mentors, members and learners to accomplish the project.
Note: It is good to know that an increase in the number of individuals resulted in less percentage of benefits for the supervisor.

Project management by supervisor

During the project, The supervisor must update the company from progression in the project. Also, the supervisor commits to accomplish the project on time. If the supervisor needs more time to carry the project out, he must request the company.

How to Suggest Research Project

Supervisors can define their own projects in the company. To do this, they should go to the “Suggest Research Project” section through their dashboard. This section includes a form to define a project. The form consists of different parts: relevant title, description of the project including start date, end date, project supervisors, requirements and support. After the company accepts the proposal, project status will be changed to a new project and everybod y can observe and request it in their dashboard.

Who can be a supervisor?

Experienced scientists with high academic degrees can be considered supervisors. Supervisors must hire and lead the members to accomplish the projects. They must also present a proposal to get the project or define a research project. TECVICO will invite these supervisors to review proposals, the papers, reports, etc.

Which supervisors/members can undertake a project?

TECVICO considers an experienced supervisor as a contractor. Supervisors can define some projects in the project section. These supervisors must present a proposal and then TECVICO will evaluate the proposal. If the result of evaluation is positive, the supervisor can start the project via spending some responsibility and knowledge coins. Applicants can also apply for these projects via spending their mentioned coins. Some projects can be funded via some industrial companies, hospitals etc. at the beginning of the project or during doing the project. Thus, these projects are called funded projects . Small industrial projects are associated with the project section. Supervisors can also apply for these projects via presenting proposals. Finally, the supervisor is able to undertake the project via company’s approval. Meanwhile, the new projects will be noticed via member dashboard or news section or social media.
