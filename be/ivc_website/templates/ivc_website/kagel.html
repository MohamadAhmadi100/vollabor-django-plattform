Hecktor 2021 Competition: HEad and neCK TumOR segmentation and outcome prediction in PET-CT images

Competition Description :

A team was sent from TECVICO to participate in this competition. The following is an explanation of the competition topic and the performance of the TECVICO team. Competition Description Five times more deadly than the flu, COVID-19 causes significant morbidity and mortality. Like other pneumonia, a lung infection with COVID-19 leads to inflammation and fluid in the lungs. COVID-19 looks very similar to other viral and bacterial pneumonia on chest x-rays, making it difficult to diagnose. Their computer vision model to detect and localize COVID-19 would help doctors make a quick and safe diagnosis. This would allow patients to get the right treatment before the virus' most serious effects occur. Currently, COVID-19 can be diagnosed using a polymerase chain reaction to detect genetic material from the virus or a chest x-ray. However, it can take a few hours and sometimes days for the molecular test results to come back. In contrast, chest x-rays can be taken in minutes. While there are guidelines to help radiologists differentiate COVID-19 from other types of infection, their assessments vary.

In addition, non-radiologists could be supported in better localizing the disease, for example with a visual bounding box. As the leading healthcare organization in its field, the Society for Imaging Informatics in Medicine (SIIM) aims to advance medical imaging informatics through education, research and innovation. SIIM has partnered with the Valencia Region Health and Biomedical Research Foundation (FISABIO), the Valencia Region Medical Imaging Database (BIMCV) and the Radiological Society of North America (RSNA) for this competition. In this competition, it will identified and located COVID-19 anomalies on chest x-rays. Specifically, categorize the x-rays as negative for pneumonia or typical, indeterminate, or atypical for COVID-19. The proposed model work with imaging data and annotations from a group of radiologists. If successful, it will help radiologists diagnose the millions of COVID-19 patients more safely and quickly. This also helps doctors understand the severity of the condition and make treatment decisions. Depending on the severity, affected patients may need hospitalization, admission to an intensive care unit, or supportive therapies such as mechanical ventilation. With a better diagnosis, more patients will quickly get the best care for their condition, which could mitigate the most serious effects of the virus.

TECVICO Team

In TECVICO, our team consists of three groups. First group worked on preprocessing of data which it was the x-ray images of lung belong to the patients. The other groups as image classification and object detection had the tasks for predictions of both a study (multi-image) and image level. In the study level task, classification of the x-ray images done for prediction of labels which may contain more than one label. They are as follows: "negative", "typical", "indeterminate", "atypical". In the image level task, detection of the desired object as “opacity” with a confidence score and bounding box should be predicted. The final result of our team was the accuracy of 0.597 vs 0.635 as top result of leaderboard in this challenge and got the 235 rank between 1305 teams.
