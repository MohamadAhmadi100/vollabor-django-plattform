Hecktor 2021 Competition: HEad and neCK TumOR segmentation and outcome prediction in PET-CT images

After the success of the first HECKTOR challenge in 2020, this year another challenge was presented at the 24th International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI) on September 27th, 2021. The competition was about HEad and neCK TumOR segmentation and outcome prediction in PET-CT images and a team was sent from TECVICO to participate in this competition. The following is an explanation of the competition topic and the performance of the TECVICO team.

Competition Description :

A team was sent from TECVICO to participate in this competition. The following is an explanation of the competition topic and the performance of the TECVICO team. Head and neck cancer (H&N) are among the most common types of cancer worldwide (fifth in terms of incidence) (Parkin et al. 2005). Radiation therapy in combination with cetuximab has established itself as the standard therapy (Bonner et al. 2010). Locoregional failures remain a major challenge, however, and occur in up to 40% of patients in the first two years after treatment (Chajon et al. 2013). Recently, several radiomics studies based on positron emission tomography (PET) and computed tomography (CT) have been proposed to better identify patients with a poorer prognosis in a non-invasive way and to make images such as these available for diagnosis and treatment to use. Planning (Vallières et al. 2017), (Bogowicz et al. 2017), (Castelli et al. 2017). Although very promising, these methods have been validated in 100-400 patients. Further validation on larger cohorts (e.g. 300-3000 patients) is required to ensure an appropriate relationship between the number of variables and observations in order to avoid overestimating the generalization performance

Such a validation requires the manual delineation of primary tumors and lymph node metastases for each patient and in three dimensions, which is persistent and error-prone. Methods for automated lesion segmentation in medical images have been proposed in various contexts, which often achieve an expert-level performance (Heimann and Meinzer 2009), (Menze et al. 2015). A surprisingly small number of studies examined the performance of computer-assisted automated segmentation of tumor lesions in PET and CT images (Song et al. 2013), (Blanc-Durand et al. 2018), (Moe et al. 2019). In 2020 we organized the first HECKTOR challenge and offered participants the opportunity to develop automatic bimodal approaches for the 3D segmentation of H&N tumors in PET / CT scans with a focus on oropharyngeal cancer. After good participation and promising results at Challenge 2020, we are now increasing the data set by 76 new cases from another clinical center with a different PET / CT scanner model and the associated reconstruction settings (CHU Milétrie, Poitiers, France). In addition, we are expanding the scope of the challenge by adding an additional task with the aim of predicting the outcome based on the PET / CT images. A clinically relevant endpoint that can be used to personalize patient management at diagnosis is being considered: the prediction of progression-free survival in H&N oropharyngeal cancer. By focusing on metabolic and morphological tissue properties, the PET and CT modalities include complementary and synergistic information for the segmentation of cancerous lesions as well as tumor features that are relevant for predicting patient outcome. In order to extract and use this information in the best possible way, modern image analysis methods have to be developed. The data used in this challenge are multicenter and include a total of four centers in Canada (Vallières et al. 2017), one center in Switzerland (Castelli et al. 2017) and one center in France (Hatt et al. 2019; Legot.) . et al. 2018) in a total of 330 patients with annotated primary tumors. This Challenge included in three tasks as follow: • Task 1: the automatic segmentation of head and neck (H&N) primary tumors in FDG-PET and CT images; • Task 2: Predict patient outcomes, namely Progression Free Survival (PFS) from the FDG-PET / CT images and available clinical data; • Task 3: The prediction of the PFS (like task 2) from the FDG-PET / CT images and available clinical data, with the exception that the ground truth annotations of primary tumors are used as inputs for the Challenger algorithms by Docker Will be provided.

TECVICO Team

Our team for Hecktor challenge consist of three groups as the three main processes for solving most of AI problems. Firstly, the preprocessing group tried to normalize of 3D head and neck images and fuse the two CT and PET images belong to the patients to generate new images witch have the details from both types of image including head and neck information. The other two groups concentrated to survival prediction from predicted binary 3D images as segmented areas for tumors each in separated task. The methods which have been used in developing proposed methods presented in the 2021 MICCAI conference and will be in access. The final result of our efforts resulted as 68% accuracy for survival prediction and got the fourth rank in the leadership in comparison with the first rank of 71% as Qurit_Tecvico team.
